P L A C E S

Dabong Railway Station

The Dabong Railway Station is located in Kelantan's Kuala Krai District. The station was named after the town it serves, and KTM Berhad is primarily responsible for its operation. Dabong is a small town that is renowned for the nearby Gua Ikan (Fish Cave) on the Galas River. The town is also the primary access point to the Gunung Stong (Mount Stong) recreational forest and the Stong Waterfall. The Dabong train station provides KTM Intercity East Coast Line service between JB Sentral and Tumpat, Gua Musang and Tumpat, Dabong and Tumpat, and vice versa. There are only a handful of daily trips served at Dabong Railway Station, and the services are operating on schedule. The lengthy route between JB Sentral and Tumpat is only serviced once per day, with only two train stops in Dabong. Additionally, Dabong station serves as the terminus for the Tumpat, Dabong route, which stops at 13 stations along the way.

Fish Cave

Gua Ikan, which translates to "Fish Cave," is believed to be so-named because a stream flows through it, which may contain fish. Gua Ikan is a recreational park that is easily accessible. There are fundamental conveniences, easily identifiable landmarks and directions, and a good road route. There are adequate, clear, and noticeable signs present. The area will be ideal for caving, camping, and cycling because the camping grounds are large, flat, and situated in a secure environment. Enjoying the beauty of the flora and fauna is also a fascinating activity that can be performed there. This location has always attracted and been preferred by a large number of people, particularly civilians and possibly tourists.

Jelawang Waterfall

Jelawang waterfall or sometimes known as the Stong waterfall is one of the highest waterfalls in South East Asia and is found in the Gunung Stong State Forest Park. A trip to the Gunung Stong State Forest Park lets you experience an all rounded trek with caving, river and mountain treks. The 21,950 ha Gunung Stong State Forest Park is gazetted on 8 November 2007 with hill dipterocarp forest below 1000 m and upper dipterocarp forest and oak-laurel forest above that. The Jelawang waterfall which stands over 300m high can be seen from a distance as you travel towards the park entrance. The biggest attraction for most people that come here is the seven-tiered, 305 metre-high Jelawang waterfall. There are accommodation facilities at the base of the waterfall.

Camp Baha

Camp Baha is located on Mount Baha. Mount Baha has Hill Dipterocarp forests, Upper Dipterocarp forests, and Montane forests. There is a lot of flora in this mountain that has medicinal value. In the vicinity of Mount Baha there are various species of flora and fauna including species that are not found anywhere and only found in the Jelawang Forest area. There are many extreme activities in this camp.
